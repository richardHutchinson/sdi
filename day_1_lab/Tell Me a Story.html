Tell Me a Story

As a developer, you will at times need to involve the creative side of your brain in problem-solving and app development. So, this activity is designed to get that creative side working.

Objectives & Outcomes

Successful completion of this activity will show that you can:
  • Write a story that provides some insight about you or your interests.
  • Simplify the story into a concise bulleted step by step list.

Level of Effort

This activity should take approximately 30m to complete. It will require:

  • 5m Research
  • 5m Prep & Delivery
  • 20m Work

If you find that this activity takes you significantly less or more time than this estimate, please contact me for guidance.

Instructions

For this assignment you will need to think of a story that has a connection to your life. It could be an adventure, a hobby, your job, a skill that you have, or anything of the like. You will then take that story, and tell it just a few sentences. As such, the story should be short and simple and not need any great amount of backstory.

This story is very similar to the way you may receive the specifics of an app you'll be working on. As with any word problem, the first thing you should do is break it down into it's important points, so you'll do the same thing with your story. You will break it down into a bulleted list of steps. Each bullet should contain only a few words and a single action item or decision point. So, if a decision is being made, it should be followed by a couple of actions that might take place based on the decision that's made.

Possible stories might incldue:

  • A day at work
  • Preparing a meal
  • Getting ready for a trip

The story should include at least 3 action items and 1 decisions point which results in various action items. An action item could be anything from "The sun rises off the starboard bow." to "I tell the crew to swab the deck." A decision point may be something like "Ahoy mateys its time for an inspection! Let's see if this deck is ready for sailin." resulting in the possible action items "Ye be walking the plank if it ain't!" or "We be drinking some rum if it are!"

You may choose to use the same story you will be using for your first deliverable on Thursday, but it is not required. That said, this is a good opportunity to test out your assignment story and receive some feedback. The purpose of this assignment is to help you in learning how to formulate a story and simplify it into manageable pieces. (Pro Tip: The week 1 deliverable has 3 decision points and at least 5 action items.)
